The Neighborhood

A name for blocks that always had one.

For years, the handful of blocks rising north from Canal Street were described by everything they were near and nothing they were. To the south sat Dimes Square; to the west, Chinatown; to the east, the Lower East Side. The streets in between — busy, lived-in, unmistakably their own — went unnamed.

NoDi gives that ground its due. Short for North of Dimes Square, it recognizes a small, dense, and genuinely distinct corner of Lower Manhattan: a place with its own rhythm, its own corners, and a community that has been here all along. It is not an expansion of any other neighborhood. It is a neighborhood.

The Neighborhood Association of NoDi exists to document that identity, support the residents and businesses within its boundaries, and make the case — clearly and confidently — that NoDi belongs on the map of New York City.

Where It Is

The boundaries of NoDi.

Compact by design. NoDi occupies the blocks directly north of Dimes Square, framed by four streets and no ambiguity.

  • NorthGrand Street
  • SouthCanal Street
  • WestAllen Street
  • EastEssex Street

The southern edge along Canal Street is shared with Dimes Square — the threshold from which NoDi takes both its bearing and its name. Everything north of that line, up to Grand, is NoDi.

Why It Matters

Small enough to know. Distinct enough to name.

01

Identity

A neighborhood earns its name through the life inside it — the regulars, the routines, the corners people mean when they say "around here." NoDi has all of it.

02

Community

Long-time residents, family-run storefronts, and newer neighbors share these blocks. NoDi is a framework for them to be recognized together, on their own terms.

03

Continuity

Naming a place is how a city remembers it. NoDi is an argument for permanence — for blocks that deserve to be known by what they are, not what they border.
